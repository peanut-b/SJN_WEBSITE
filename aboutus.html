Our Historical Journey

From humble beginnings to a beacon of faith in Malibay, Pasay City

Original structure of San Juan Nepomuceno Parish

A Legacy of Faith and Community

San Juan Nepomuceno Parish stands as a testament to the enduring faith of the Malibay community. Established in the heart of Pasay City, our church has witnessed generations of prayer, celebration, and devotion.

Named after Saint John Nepomucene, the patron saint of confession and the protector against floods, our parish has served as a spiritual sanctuary for over a century, guiding the faithful through times of joy, challenge, and transformation.

Historical Timeline

Early 1900s

The Humble Beginnings

The first chapel was established in Malibay, serving as a small worship space for the growing Catholic community. Initially, Masses were celebrated by visiting priests from nearby parishes.

1935

Official Parish Establishment

San Juan Nepomuceno was officially recognized as a parish by the Archdiocese of Manila. The first resident parish priest was assigned, marking the beginning of organized parish life in Malibay.

1945

Surviving World War II

The church served as a refuge during the liberation of Manila. Despite the destruction around Malibay, the church building remained standing, becoming a symbol of hope and resilience for the community.

1960s

Major Renovations

Significant renovations expanded the church to accommodate the growing congregation. The bell tower was completed, and additional facilities were built to support various ministries.

1980

Community Development

Establishment of various parish organizations and ministries including the Parish Pastoral Council, Social Action Ministry, and Youth Ministry, strengthening community involvement.

2000

Centennial Celebrations

The parish celebrated its centennial with year-long activities including historical exhibits, special Masses, and community outreach programs. A time capsule was buried for future generations.

Present Day

Continuing the Legacy

Today, San Juan Nepomuceno Parish continues to serve as a vibrant center of faith, offering daily Masses, sacraments, and numerous programs for spiritual growth and community service.

Architectural Heritage

Sanctuary & Altar

The main altar features intricate wood carvings depicting scenes from the life of Saint John Nepomucene. The tabernacle is a masterpiece of local craftsmanship.

Stained Glass Windows

Beautiful stained glass windows installed in the 1970s depict biblical scenes and saints, creating a kaleidoscope of colors during morning Masses.

Historic Bell Tower

The 50-foot bell tower houses three bells, the largest of which was cast in 1938. The bells continue to call the faithful to worship every day.
